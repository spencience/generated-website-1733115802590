Brown Bears (Ursus arctos)

Brown bears are among the most widespread bear species. They're known for their impressive size and adaptability across various habitats.

  • Weight: 180-360 kg (males)
  • Habitat: Forests, mountains, tundra
  • Diet: Omnivorous
Learn more about Brown Bears →

Conservation Status

Least Concern - Though populations are stable in many areas, habitat loss remains a concern.

American Black Bears (Ursus americanus)

Black bears are the most common bear species in North America, known for their intelligence and adaptability to human presence.

  • Weight: 130-280 kg (males)
  • Habitat: Forests, urban interfaces
  • Diet: Omnivorous, opportunistic
Learn more about Black Bears →

Did You Know?

Despite their name, black bears can be brown, cinnamon, or even white in color!
