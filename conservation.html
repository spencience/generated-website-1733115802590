Current Conservation Status

Of the world's eight bear species, six are currently listed as vulnerable or endangered. Climate change, habitat loss, and human conflict pose significant threats to bear populations globally.

Critical Numbers

  • Giant Pandas: ~1,864 in the wild
  • Polar Bears: 22,000-31,000 remaining
  • Sun Bears: Population unknown, rapidly declining

Success Stories

Thanks to conservation efforts, some bear populations are showing signs of recovery. The Giant Panda was downlisted from "endangered" to "vulnerable" in 2016.

Major Threats

Habitat Loss

Deforestation and urban development continue to fragment bear habitats worldwide.

Climate Change

Rising temperatures affect hibernation patterns and food availability.

Human Conflict

Increasing human-bear interactions lead to conflicts and bear deaths.

Active Conservation Programs

Protected Areas

Establishment and maintenance of national parks and wildlife reserves.

Breeding Programs

Captive breeding and rehabilitation centers for endangered species.

Research Initiatives

Scientific studies on bear behavior, habitat needs, and population dynamics.
